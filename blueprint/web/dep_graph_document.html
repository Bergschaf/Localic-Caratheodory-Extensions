Definition 3\(f^*\) and \(f_*\)

For every continuous Function \(f : X \rightarrow Y\) between topological Spaces, there exists a pair of functors \((f^*,f_*)\).

\begin{gather*} f* = f^{-1} : O(Y) \rightarrow O(X)\\ f_* : O(X) \rightarrow O(Y) \coloneqq A \mapsto \bigcup _{f^*(v) \le A} v\\ \end{gather*}

Definition 8Nucleus

A nucleus is a map \(e : O(E) \rightarrow O(E)\) with the following three properties:

  1. \(e\) is idempotent

  2. \(U \le e U\)

  3. \(e(U \cap V) = e(U) \cap e(V)\)

Lemma 7Embedding

(Leroy Lemme 1) \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) is an embedding, if at least one of the following arguemnts holds:

  1. \(f^*\) is surjective

  2. \(f_*\) is injective

  3. \(f^{*}f_* = 1_{O(X)})\)

Lemma 12factorisation

(Leroy Lemme 2) Let \(i : X \rightarrow E\) be an embedding and \(f: Y \rightarrow E\) be a morphism of spaces. To have \(f\) factor through \(i\), it is necessary and sufficient that \(i_*i^*(V) \le f_*f^*(V) for all V \in O(E)\).

Lemma 13Familiy of subspaces

(Leroy CH 4) Let \(X_i\) be a family of subspaces of \(E\) and \(e_i\) be the corresponding nuclei. For every \(V \in O(E)\), let \(e(V)\) be the onion of all \(W \in O(E)\) which are contained in every \(e_i(V)\). Then

  1. \(e\) is the corresponding nuclus of a subspace \(X\) of \(E\)

  2. a subspace \(Z\) of \(E\) conatains \(x\) if and only if it containd all \(X_i\) x is thus called the union of \(X_i\) denoted by \(\bigcup _i X_i\)
